## SCOPE OF AUTHORITY

Upon a triggering event (as defined in Section 8.3 of the Bylaws), the Designated Successor shall immediately assume the following authority:

### Technical Authority
- Full administrative access to all source code repositories (GitHub, GitLab, etc.)
- Authority to approve, merge, or revert pull requests
- Authority to issue emergency security patches
- Authority to modify or create Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) following the MIP process
- Authority to appoint, direct, or remove technical co-maintainers

### Operational Authority
- Authority to communicate with stakeholders, community members, and the press
- Authority to convene emergency Board meetings
- Authority to execute contracts under $10,000 without Board approval
- Authority to hire contractors and consultants within approved budget
- Authority to represent the Corporation in technical standards bodies and conferences

### Financial Authority (Interim)
- Access to operating bank accounts for routine expenses
- Authority to authorize emergency expenditures up to $5,000
- Authority to approve payroll and vendor payments

**Limitation:** Contracts exceeding $10,000, policy changes (licensing, governance), and budget amendments require Board approval.

---

## TRIGGERING EVENTS

This designation becomes effective immediately upon any of the following events:

1. **Death** of the Lead Architect
2. **Permanent Incapacity** as certified by two licensed physicians in writing
3. **Written Resignation** with immediate effect (resignation effective at a future date does not trigger this designation)
4. **Removal by the Board** under extraordinary circumstances as provided in the Bylaws

---

## BOARD CONFIRMATION REQUIREMENT

Within five (5) business days of assuming authority, the Designated Successor shall:
- Notify all Board members in writing of the triggering event
- Convene an emergency Board meeting (in-person or virtual)
- Present a transition plan and request Board confirmation

The Board shall vote to confirm the Designated Successor as permanent President/Lead Architect or appoint an interim President and initiate a search process.

---

## KNOWLEDGE TRANSFER COMMITMENTS

To ensure effective succession, I commit to the following knowledge transfer practices:

1. **Quarterly Briefings:** Conduct quarterly briefings with the Designated Successor on strategic initiatives, ongoing projects, and key relationships.

2. **Annual Succession Drills:** Conduct at least one (1) simulated succession exercise annually to test communication protocols, access procedures, and Board response.

3. **Shared Access:** Grant the Designated Successor read-only access to critical systems, repositories, and communication channels (with audit logging enabled).

4. **Documentation:** Maintain current documentation of:
   - Administrative credentials (in secure vault accessible to Designated Successor and Secretary)
   - Key stakeholder contacts
   - Ongoing contracts and commitments
   - Strategic roadmap and priorities

5. **Co-Maintainer Introductions:** Introduce the Designated Successor to all co-maintainers, advisors, and key community members.
