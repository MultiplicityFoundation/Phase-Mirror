# Entity Formation Decision

**Date:** January 28, 2026  
**Decision Authority:** Founder/Incorporator  
**Status:** Locked

## Summary

Citizen Gardens Foundation will be formed as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit corporation in the State of Ohio.

## Locked Decisions

### 1. Entity Type
**Decision:** 501(c)(3) nonprofit corporation  
**Rationale:** 
- **Permanence**: Foundation cannot be sold or privatized
- **Tax Advantage**: No federal or state income tax
- **Signaling**: Commitment to public goods, not shareholder returns
- **Regulatory Moat**: Enhanced trust with enterprises and regulators

**Alternative Considered:**
- **Benefit Corporation**: Rejected due to state-dependency (Delaware statute could change)
- **LLC with Foundation License**: Rejected due to privatization risk (heirs could monetize)
- **Cooperative**: Rejected due to voting overhead and governance complexity

### 2. State of Incorporation
**Decision:** Ohio  
**Rationale:**
- Founder residency
- Clear nonprofit statutes
- Lower filing fees ($99 vs $300+ in other states)
- No annual report fees

### 3. Entity Name
**Decision:** Citizen Gardens Foundation  
**Rationale:**
- Reflects public goods mission
- No existing conflicts in Ohio SOS database
- Aligns with Phase Mirror, QAGI, and Multiplicity projects

### 4. Structure
**Decision:** Non-stock corporation with Board governance  
**Rationale:**
- No shares = no equity distribution
- Board provides accountability without voting overhead
- Founder can appoint initial directors
- Succession mechanism via bylaws

## Protected Invariants

The following principles are inscribed in Article V of the Articles of Incorporation:

1. **Public Access**: All core specifications (QAGI, Multiplicity, Phase Mirror) shall be publicly accessible at no cost under permissive open license (Apache 2.0 minimum)

2. **Trademark Protection**: All trademarks owned by the Foundation shall be used solely to protect the integrity of the specifications and implementations

3. **Non-Distribution**: No part of net earnings shall inure to the benefit of any private shareholder or individual

4. **Mission Preservation**: Upon dissolution, all assets shall transfer to another 501(c)(3) organization with similar mission
