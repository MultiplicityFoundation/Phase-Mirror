# Bylaws of Citizen Gardens Foundation
## A Nonprofit Corporation

**Adopted:** [Date]  
**Last Amended:** [Date]

---

## ARTICLE I - NAME AND PURPOSE

### Section 1.1 - Name
The name of this corporation is **Citizen Gardens Foundation** (the "Corporation").

### Section 1.2 - Purpose
The Corporation is organized as a nonprofit corporation under Ohio law exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, as set forth in the Articles of Incorporation.

---

## ARTICLE II - OFFICES

### Section 2.1 - Principal Office
The principal office of the Corporation shall be located in the State of Ohio. The Board of Directors may change the location of the principal office from time to time.

### Section 2.2 - Registered Office
The Corporation shall continuously maintain a registered office and registered agent in Ohio as required by law.

---

## ARTICLE III - BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 3.1 - Powers
The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by the Board of Directors, which shall have all powers permitted by law and the Articles of Incorporation.

### Section 3.2 - Number and Qualifications
The Board shall consist of not less than one (1) and not more than nine (9) directors. Directors need not be residents of Ohio. The exact number of directors shall be fixed by resolution of the Board.

### Section 3.3 - Term of Office
Directors shall serve for a term of three (3) years or until their successors are elected and qualified. Terms shall be staggered to ensure continuity.

### Section 3.4 - Election and Appointment
- Initial directors are appointed by the Incorporator
- Subsequent directors are elected by majority vote of the existing Board
- Directors may serve consecutive terms without limit

### Section 3.5 - Removal
A director may be removed, with or without cause, by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the remaining directors at a meeting called for that purpose, provided:
- The director to be removed receives at least 10 days' written notice
- The director is given an opportunity to be heard at the meeting
- Removal does not violate any contractual or statutory rights

### Section 3.6 - Vacancies
Vacancies on the Board, including vacancies resulting from removal or resignation, shall be filled by a majority vote of the remaining directors, even if less than a quorum. A director elected to fill a vacancy serves until the next election of directors.

### Section 3.7 - Resignation
A director may resign at any time by delivering written notice to the President or Secretary. Resignation is effective upon receipt unless a later date is specified.

---

## ARTICLE IV - MEETINGS OF THE BOARD

### Section 4.1 - Regular Meetings
The Board shall hold at least one (1) regular meeting annually. Additional regular meetings may be scheduled by Board resolution.

### Section 4.2 - Special Meetings
Special meetings may be called by:
- The President
- Any two (2) directors
- One-third (1/3) of the directors then in office

### Section 4.3 - Notice
Notice of all meetings shall be given at least 5 days in advance by mail, email, or other electronic means, stating the time, place, and purpose of the meeting. Notice may be waived in writing by any director.

### Section 4.4 - Quorum
A majority of directors then in office constitutes a quorum for the transaction of business. If a quorum is not present, the meeting may be adjourned without further notice.

### Section 4.5 - Voting
Each director has one vote. Actions require a majority vote of directors present at a meeting with a quorum, except where these Bylaws or law requires a higher threshold.

### Section 4.6 - Remote Participation
Directors may participate in meetings by telephone, video conference, or similar technology, provided all participants can hear each other. Participation by such means constitutes presence in person.

### Section 4.7 - Action Without Meeting
Any action required or permitted to be taken at a Board meeting may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing (including by email).

---

## ARTICLE V - OFFICERS

### Section 5.1 - Officers
The officers of the Corporation shall be:
- President (or Lead Architect)
- Vice President (optional)
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Such other officers as the Board may appoint

One person may hold multiple offices except that the President and Secretary shall not be the same person.

### Section 5.2 - Election and Term
Officers are elected by the Board and serve at the pleasure of the Board. Officers hold office until removed, resign, or until successors are elected.

### Section 5.3 - President / Lead Architect
The President (also known as Lead Architect for technical leadership roles) shall:
- Preside at Board meetings
- Execute documents on behalf of the Corporation
- Supervise the Corporation's activities and operations
- Make day-to-day technical and operational decisions
- Appoint co-maintainers and technical staff
- Have authority to approve emergency protocol changes

### Section 5.4 - Secretary
The Secretary shall:
- Keep minutes of Board meetings
- Maintain corporate records
- Give notice of meetings as required
- Certify and maintain custody of corporate documents

### Section 5.5 - Treasurer
The Treasurer shall:
- Oversee financial affairs of the Corporation
- Maintain financial records
- Present financial reports to the Board
- Ensure compliance with financial reporting requirements

### Section 5.6 - Removal
Officers may be removed by majority vote of the Board at any time, with or without cause.

---

## ARTICLE VI - COMMITTEES

### Section 6.1 - Establishment
The Board may establish committees (standing or special) by resolution, delegating such powers as the Board deems appropriate, except powers that by law may not be delegated.

### Section 6.2 - Certification Committee
A Certification Committee shall be established to:
- Review proposed ADRs (Architecture Decision Records)
- Vote on Multiplicity Improvement Proposals (MIPs)
- Approve certification standards for implementations
- Consist of at least two (2) domain experts appointed by the Board

---

## ARTICLE VII - FISCAL YEAR AND FINANCIAL MATTERS

### Section 7.1 - Fiscal Year
The fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year (January 1 - December 31), unless otherwise determined by the Board.

### Section 7.2 - Contracts and Loans
The Board may authorize officers to enter into contracts, borrow money, and execute instruments on behalf of the Corporation.

### Section 7.3 - Deposits and Accounts
All funds shall be deposited in banks or other depositories approved by the Board.

### Section 7.4 - Annual Budget
The Treasurer shall present an annual budget to the Board for approval.

---

## ARTICLE VIII - SUCCESSION AND CONTINUITY (CRITICAL)

### Section 8.1 - Purpose and Importance
This Article establishes procedures to ensure continuity of leadership, protection of the mission, and operational stability in the event of the Lead Architect's death, incapacity, or resignation. It is a lynchpin of organizational resilience.

### Section 8.2 - Successor Designation
The Lead Architect (President) shall designate in writing a named successor (the "Designated Successor") by filing a Designation Instrument with the Secretary. This instrument shall include:
- Name and contact information of the Designated Successor
- Date of designation
- Scope of authority granted upon succession
- Any limitations or conditions

The Lead Architect may update the Designation Instrument at any time by filing a new instrument with the Secretary.

### Section 8.3 - Triggering Events
Succession under this Article is triggered by:
- Death of the Lead Architect
- Permanent incapacity (as certified by two licensed physicians)
- Written resignation effective immediately
- Removal by the Board under extraordinary circumstances

### Section 8.4 - Immediate Authority
Upon a triggering event, the Designated Successor shall immediately assume the following authority:
- Operational control of all technical systems and repositories
- Authority to approve emergency security patches
- Authority to communicate with stakeholders and the public
- Interim President powers until Board confirmation

### Section 8.5 - Board Confirmation
Within five (5) business days of assuming authority, the Designated Successor shall:
- Notify all Board members of the triggering event
- Convene an emergency Board meeting (in-person or remote)
- Present a transition plan

The Board shall vote to:
- Confirm the Designated Successor as permanent President/Lead Architect (majority vote required), or
- Appoint an interim President and initiate a search process (two-thirds vote required)

### Section 8.6 - Role Clarity
The Designated Successor, upon confirmation, shall have the following authorities:

**Technical Authority:**
- Approve or reject pull requests to core repositories
- Merge security patches without delay
- Modify architecture decision records (ADRs) following MIP process
- Appoint and remove technical co-maintainers

**Operational Authority:**
- Execute contracts under $10,000 without Board approval
- Hire contractors and consultants within approved budget
- Represent the Corporation in technical standards bodies

**Financial Authority:**
- Access operating accounts for routine expenses
- Authorize emergency expenditures up to $5,000
- Contracts above $10,000 require Board approval

**Policy Authority:**
- Policy changes (licensing, governance, ADR amendments) require Board vote
- Emergency protocol changes (security, availability) may be made immediately with Board notification within 24 hours

### Section 8.7 - Co-Maintainer Knowledge Transfer
The Lead Architect shall maintain a documented knowledge transfer process, including:
- Quarterly briefings with the Designated Successor
- Annual succession drills (simulated transition)
- Shared access to critical systems (with audit logging)
- Documentation of all administrative credentials in a secure vault accessible to Designated Successor and Secretary

### Section 8.8 - Backup Succession
If the Designated Successor is unavailable, unwilling, or unable to serve, the Board shall appoint an interim President within 10 business days. During the interim period, the Secretary assumes operational authority to prevent service disruption.

### Section 8.9 - Public Communication Protocol
Within 48 hours of a triggering event, the Designated Successor (or interim President) shall:
- Publish a public statement confirming the transition
- Reassure stakeholders that operations continue uninterrupted
- Provide a timeline for any governance changes

This transparency prevents speculation and maintains community trust.

## ARTICLE IX - CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

### Section 9.1 - Policy
Directors and officers shall avoid conflicts of interest and must disclose any potential conflict before the Board votes on a related matter.

### Section 9.2 - Disclosure
Any director or officer with a financial or personal interest in a transaction must:
- Disclose the interest to the Board in writing
- Abstain from voting on the matter
- Not be counted toward a quorum for that vote

### Section 9.3 - Annual Statements
Each director and officer shall annually complete a conflict of interest disclosure statement.

---

## ARTICLE X - INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 10.1 - Indemnification
The Corporation shall indemnify directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by Ohio law, provided such indemnification does not jeopardize the Corporation's tax-exempt status.

### Section 10.2 - Insurance
The Corporation may purchase insurance on behalf of any person entitled to indemnification.

---

## ARTICLE XI - AMENDMENT OF BYLAWS

### Section 11.1 - Power to Amend
These Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors at any meeting, provided:
- Notice of the proposed amendment is given at least 10 days in advance
- Amendment does not conflict with the Articles of Incorporation or law

### Section 11.2 - Protected Provisions
Amendments to Article VIII (Succession and Continuity) require unanimous Board approval to ensure continuity safeguards remain robust.

---

## ARTICLE XII - DISSOLUTION

### Section 12.1 - Voluntary Dissolution
The Corporation may be dissolved by:
- Two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board
- Approval by the Ohio Attorney General (if required)
- Compliance with all legal requirements

### Section 12.2 - Asset Distribution
Upon dissolution, all assets shall be distributed in accordance with Article VI of the Articles of Incorporation (to other 501(c)(3) organizations with similar mission).
